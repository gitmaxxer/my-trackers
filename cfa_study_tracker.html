LES vs Schweser
When to use each platform, by topic
Platform usage guide
TopicPrimaryReasoning
Ethics (R89–93)LES-firstCFA Institute writes exam Ethics questions from official text. Nuance and case studies are lost in Schweser summaries. Read the Standards of Practice Handbook multiple times in LES.
FSA (R27–38)LES-firstOfficial curriculum has annotated financial statements and worked problems essential for beginners to build intuition. Schweser only for Key Concepts review.
Fixed Income (R47–65)LES-firstCalculation-heavy. LES step-by-step examples for bond pricing, duration, convexity, and yield are clearer than Schweser's condensed treatment.
Derivatives (R66–75)Both equallySchweser for conceptual framework; LES for worked pricing problems (forwards, options valuation, put-call parity, binomial model).
Quant Methods (R1–11)Schweser-firstModule-by-module format with quizzes builds quantitative intuition efficiently. Use LES only to clarify specific unclear concepts.
Economics (R12–19)Schweser-firstDescriptive and conceptual. Schweser covers exam-relevant content without the added reading time of the full LES curriculum.
Corporate Issuers (R20–26)Schweser-firstConceptual. Schweser summaries are sufficient. Low LES value-add for this topic.
Equity (R39–46)Schweser-firstPrimarily conceptual and descriptive. Schweser's structure suits exam prep well.
Alt Investments (R76–82)Schweser-firstDescriptive topics. Schweser handles the exam-relevant content well.
Portfolio Mgmt (R83–88)Schweser-firstMix of conceptual and math. Use LES for CAPM / efficient frontier worked examples only.
Mock Exams & Final ReviewLES-firstLES mock exams are written by CFA Institute and most closely mirror the real exam. Always prioritise LES mocks over third-party versions.
